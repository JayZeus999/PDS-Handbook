"Oliver Twist" follows the journey of an orphan boy navigating poverty, crime, and eventual redemption in 19th-century England.
Early Life and Workhouse
Oliver Twist is born in a workhouse in 1830s England, and his mother dies shortly after his birth, leaving him an orphan. He spends his early years in a poorly run orphanage and later in a workhouse for adults, where he suffers neglect and abuse. After daring to ask for more food, Oliver is punished and apprenticed to Mr. Sowerberry, an undertaker. Conflicts with another apprentice, Noah Claypole, lead Oliver to run away to London.

Life in London and Criminal Underworld
Near London, Oliver meets Jack Dawkins, the Artful Dodger, who introduces him to Fagin, a criminal who trains boys to pick pockets. Oliver is reluctantly involved in a theft but is caught and mistakenly accused. 
He is rescued by Mr. Brownlow, an elderly gentleman who recognizes Oliver’s innocence and takes him into his care. Despite this, Oliver is later kidnapped by Nancy, an associate of Fagin and Bill Sikes, and returned to the criminal gang. 

Adventures and Escapes
Oliver is forced to participate in a burglary with Bill Sikes and Toby Crackit, which goes wrong, leaving him wounded. He is rescued and cared for by Rose Maylie and a local doctor, eventually reuniting with Mr. Brownlow. Throughout these events, Oliver repeatedly escapes danger and seeks safety, highlighting his innocence and resilience.

Resolution and Themes
The novel concludes with Oliver discovering the truth about his parentage and reconnecting with his remaining family. Dickens uses Oliver’s story to expose the harsh realities of child labor, poverty, and crime in Victorian England, while emphasizing themes of innocence, morality, and social justice. Characters like Nancy demonstrate moral complexity, showing empathy and courage despite their criminal environment.

Key Characters
Oliver Twist – The innocent and resilient orphan protagonist
Fagin – Elderly criminal who trains children to steal
Artful Dodger (Jack Dawkins) – Skilled young pickpocket
Bill Sikes – Violent criminal and Nancy’s partner
Nancy – Compassionate yet conflicted member of Fagin’s gang
Mr. Brownlow – Kind benefactor who protects Oliver
Rose Maylie – Caring young woman who aids Oliver
Oliver Twist remains a classic social novel, blending adventure with a critique of societal injustices, and has inspired numerous adaptations in film, theater, and literature.
